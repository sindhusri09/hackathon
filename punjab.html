Akaltakht

The Akal Takht, meaning "Throne of the Timeless One," is one of the five seats of temporal authority of the Sikhs. It is located in the Harmandir Sahib complex, also known as the Golden Temple, in Amritsar, Punjab, India. The Akal Takht serves as the highest political institution of the Sikhs and is considered the central place of authority for Sikh religious and political affairs. It was established by the sixth Sikh Guru, Guru Hargobind Ji, in 1606 and has historically been a symbol of Sikh sovereignty and justice.
